CONSEQUENCES OF WATER WASTAGE

Water wastage can have many consequences, including:

  • Environmental consequences: Depleting fresh water resources, increasing energy consumption, disrupting habitats, putting stress on ecosystems, affecting food production, contributing to climate change, and giving rise to tension and conflict
  • Economic consequences: Higher water bills, and energy waste
  • Health consequences: Contaminated water can cause harmful effects on human health and can lead to water borne diseases
  • wastewater pollutant levels
  • Water scarcity
  • hunger and malnutrition

Some other consequences of water wastage include:

  • Limiting availability - Wasted water may not be available to other communities, especially in areas where water shortage is common.
  • Excess energy - Excess energy will be used in sewage treatment plants to treat wasted water.
  • Air pollution - Air pollution can directly contaminate the surface of bodies of water and soil
  • Groundwater pollution - Groundwater pollution is the second most prevalent type of environmental pollution, after air pollution.
  • Chemical pollution - Chemical fertilizers and pesticides are used by farmers to protect crops from insects and bacterias
